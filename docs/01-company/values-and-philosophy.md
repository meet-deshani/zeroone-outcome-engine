## Six Core Values

### 1. Action > Advice
We deliver working code, not slides. Every engagement produces something you can run, test, and use — not a 50-page report that sits in a drawer.

### 2. Outcomes > Hours
We bill for results, not time. If the system doesn't work, you don't pay. This aligns our incentives with yours completely.

### 3. Ownership
The client owns everything — IP, code, models, data, infrastructure. You can file a patent on what we build. You can publish a research paper. Walk away anytime with everything.

### 4. Simplicity
We use boring, reliable tech that scales. No bleeding-edge experiments on your production systems. If a simpler solution works, we use it.

### 5. Small Teams
1 Project Manager + a network of AI Agents beats 10 humans for most operational tasks. We keep teams lean and let AI do the repetitive work.

### 6. Business First
If it doesn't improve your P&L, we don't build it. Every project starts with a business question: "How does this make or save money?"

## The 10-Year-Old Test

Every piece of text we produce — from documentation to website copy to reports — must be understandable by a 10-year-old. This is a hard rule.

**We write:**
- Short sentences (15 words max is ideal)
- Everyday words ("stop losing money" not "margin recovery optimization")
- Specific numbers instead of vague claims
- Clear structure with generous whitespace

**We never write:**
- Jargon without explanation
- Buzzwords like "synergy," "leverage," or "paradigm"
- Walls of text longer than 3 sentences without a break
- Dark patterns or aggressive sales language

## Design Philosophy

### Outcome-First Engagement
We don't sell hours. We sell outcomes. Every engagement has a clear deliverable and a measurable business result attached to it.

### Zero Vendor Lock-In
Everything we build is portable. You can take the code, the models, the infrastructure, and move it anywhere. No proprietary formats, no lock-in tricks.

### Privacy by Default
Data never leaves your infrastructure unless you explicitly choose to. We default to on-premise, offline-first, private-by-design architecture.

### Progressive Disclosure
We start with the simplest possible solution and add complexity only when the business demands it. Three similar lines of code is better than a premature abstraction.
